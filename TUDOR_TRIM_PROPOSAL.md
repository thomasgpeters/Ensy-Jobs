# Tudor Style Trim Proposal

**Client:** Elcy Sandora
**Date:** April 16, 2026

---

## Project Overview

Apply decorative Tudor-style trim to the white taller section of the house that wraps around to the front. The goal is to give the plain white upper walls a classic Tudor half-timber appearance using trim boards that are glued, nailed, sealed, and painted brown. A mock-up layout will be created first for Elcy's approval before permanent installation.

Reference photos of real estate Tudor homes have been provided for style guidance only — the actual layout will be customized to fit Elcy's house.

---

## Existing Conditions

- The upper story has a tall, plain white wall section that rises above the lower roofline
- This white section wraps from the side of the house around to the front
- Currently no decorative trim — flat white surface
- Brown vertical siding/panels on adjacent sections of the upper story

---

## Scope of Work

### 1. Design and Mock-Up

- Study the reference Tudor home photos for style direction (diagonal braces, horizontal beams, vertical timbers)
- Assess the white wall sections — measure dimensions on all sides that wrap to the front
- Create a mock-up layout of the Tudor trim pattern directly on the wall for Elcy to see
- Adjust the design based on Elcy's feedback until she approves the final layout

### 2. Measure and Cut Trim

- Once the layout is approved, measure and cut all trim boards to size
- Pre-paint and seal trim pieces before installation where possible
- Dry-fit pieces against the wall to confirm fit before permanent attachment

### 3. Installation — Glue and Nail

- Apply construction adhesive to the back of each trim board
- Nail each piece securely to the wall
- Ensure all joints are tight and aligned per the approved layout
- Work around any existing features (windows, gutters, roofline transitions)

### 4. Seal and Paint

- Seal all trim with exterior-grade sealer to protect against moisture and weather
- Paint all trim boards brown to match the Tudor half-timber style
- Caulk edges where trim meets the white wall for a clean, weather-tight finish
