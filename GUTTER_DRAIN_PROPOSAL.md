# Gutter with French Drain Proposal

**Client:** Elsy Sandora
**Date:** April 16, 2026

---

## Project Overview

Replace the gutter and downspout in one area of the house, repair the trim/fascia in that same area, and connect the downspout into the existing french drain. The exposed french drain line needs to be buried better for a cleaner look and more effective drainage.

---

## Existing Conditions

- Gutter and downspout run along the lower roofline where the one-story section meets the taller upper story
- Downspout comes down the white painted brick wall near the screened porch and back steps
- French drain line (black corrugated tubing) runs along the ground near the raised garden beds — currently sitting exposed on the surface
- Trim/fascia board in the gutter area is damaged and needs replacement
- Area is accessible from the backyard near the screened porch

---

## Scope of Work

### 1. Remove Old Gutter, Downspout, and Damaged Trim

- Take down the existing gutter and downspout in the affected area
- Remove damaged fascia/trim board behind the gutter
- Inspect the underlying structure for any rot or damage

### 2. Repair and Replace Trim/Fascia

- Replace damaged fascia board with new material
- Repair any rot or damage found behind the old trim
- Prime and paint the new trim to match the existing house finish

### 3. Install New Gutter and Downspout

- Mount new gutter along the repaired fascia with proper hangers
- Ensure correct slope for drainage toward the downspout location
- Install new downspout down the wall, secured with brackets
- Install end caps and seal all gutter joints

### 4. Tap into French Drain

- Connect the bottom of the downspout to the existing french drain line
- Use appropriate fittings/adapters to tie the downspout into the corrugated drain tubing
- Ensure a secure, watertight connection

### 5. Bury the French Drain Line

- Trench along the path of the exposed french drain line
- Set the drain line into the trench at proper depth and grade for drainage
- Backfill and cover the trench
- Restore the ground surface — clean up disturbed soil and gravel areas
- Test the full system: run water through the gutter and confirm it flows through the downspout, into the french drain, and out to the drainage exit
