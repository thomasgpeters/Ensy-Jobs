# Front Door Replacement Proposal

**Client:** Elcy Sandora
**Date:** April 16, 2026

---

## Project Overview

Remove the existing front door and storm door, strip back to the rough opening, and install a new fancy colonial/Victorian-style front door with sidelight. Elcy will purchase the door — we will pick it up and transport it in the F-350 (8-foot bed). Elcy has sample photos of doors she likes (not yet on file). The style will be similar to a colonial door with sidelight — decorative glass panels, traditional detailing.

---

## Existing Conditions

- Current front door is a standard 6-panel door, painted dark red/maroon
- Storm/screen door mounted in front of the main door
- One sidelight panel to the right of the door
- Door frame and surrounding trim showing wear
- Covered front porch with potted plants flanking the entry
- White painted brick exterior around the door opening

---

## Scope of Work

### 1. Material Pickup

- Pick up the new door unit from the supplier in the F-350 (8-foot bed)
- Transport carefully to the job site

### 2. Removal of Existing Door

- Remove the storm/screen door
- Remove the existing 6-panel front door
- Remove existing trim, casing, and any damaged framing
- Strip back to the rough opening
- Inspect rough opening for any rot, damage, or structural issues and repair as needed

### 3. Rough Opening Preparation

- Clean and square up the rough opening
- Verify the opening is sized correctly for the new door with sidelight unit
- Widen or adjust the rough opening if necessary to accommodate the sidelight
- Ensure the sill/threshold area is level and solid

### 4. Door Installation

- Set the new front door with sidelight unit into the rough opening
- Shim the frame for level and plumb
- Secure the frame with screws into the framing
- Insulate gaps between the frame and rough opening with low-expansion foam
- Confirm the door operates smoothly — opens, closes, and latches properly

### 5. Trim and Finish

- Install exterior trim/casing — brick mold or appropriate trim to match the house style
- Install interior trim/casing
- Install new hardware — lockset, deadbolt, hinges
- Apply weatherstripping
- Caulk and seal all exterior joints
